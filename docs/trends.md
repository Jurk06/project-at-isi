# Temporal Trend Analysis

## Overview

This section analyzes how water table depth has changed over time. By examining historical data across multiple years, we can identify long-term trends in water availability and predict future water scarcity.

## Dataset

- **Temporal Variable**: Year (from Atal Jal dataset - March 31, 2021)
- **Key Metric**: Well depth measurements
- **Scope**: Multiple years of observations across Indian states

## Analysis Methodology

### 1. Time Series Decomposition
- Separate trend, seasonal, and residual components
- Identify whether changes are permanent or cyclical

### 2. State-wise Trend Comparison
- Track how different regions are responding
- Compare rates of change across geographic areas

### 3. Statistical Testing
- Test for significant temporal trends
- Calculate slopes and growth rates
- Assess statistical significance

## Key Findings

- **Overall Trend**: Water table depth showing [upward/downward/mixed] trends
- **Regional Variation**: Some states show faster decline than others
- **Seasonal Patterns**: Monsoon and dry season variations detected
- **Acceleration**: Rate of change appears [stable/accelerating/decelerating]

## Implications

Understanding these trends helps:
- Predict future water availability
- Prioritize regions for intervention
- Inform policy decisions
- Plan groundwater management strategies
